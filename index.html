Dire Wolf

This creature's canine teeth were two to three inches long, and were thicker than those of any known canid(dog) in history.When the beast bit into its prey, these teeth were strong enough to rip through muscle and crush the bone underneath.

  • Scientific name: Canis dirus
  • Average length: up to 5 ft.
  • Average height: up to 3,5 ft.
  • Average weight: up to 200lbs.
  • Habitat: Fossils have been found in North America

The dire wolf was a stocky creature, and the biggest ”dog” ever to walk on Earth. A cousin to the modern grey wolf lived side by side with the grey wolf for thousands of years.The dire wolf became extinct about 16.000 years ago, while gray wolf is still around.
